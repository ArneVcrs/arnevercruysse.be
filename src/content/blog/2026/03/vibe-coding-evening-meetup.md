The way developers write code, test hypotheses, and build digital products has shifted dramatically since early 2025, when Andrej Karpathy coined the term **"vibe coding"** on X. Today, a new class of natural-language-driven development environments, including Cursor, Bolt.new, Lovable, Windsurf, Vercel v0, and Claude Code, has moved AI beyond simple auto-complete into the territory of active co-builders.

I attended a packed Tech&Meet evening event dedicated entirely to this phenomenon. To explore both the technical capabilities and the business implications of AI-assisted engineering, the event featured two distinct speakers:
* **Frank Damen** (Founder at 3volve Consulting & Product Manager at ASTRID nv): With 12 years of experience in product management, Frank shapes the strategic direction of control room software and public safety apps at ASTRID nv. Through 3volve Consulting, he runs specialized training courses showing product managers and non-technical stakeholders how to use AI tools to spin up high-fidelity prototypes on the fly.
* **Kenny Dendauw** (Managing Partner at Starfisk): Kenny has spent 15 years architecting and scaling web applications. At Starfisk, he coordinates a team of engineers building complex business systems, API integrations, and SaaS products.

## Frank Damen: Democratizing Software & "Building for Agreement"
Frank Damen kicked off the event by focusing on how vibe coding democratizes the creation process. With modern tools, the barrier to bringing a software idea to life has vanished. Non-technical project managers, founders, and designers can describe an interface, and the AI constructs a functioning, interactive web page in seconds.

Frank’s core philosophy is **"Think. Build. Agree."** 
Instead of teams debating for weeks over abstract text documents or flat wireframes (which are often misinterpreted), Frank uses tools like Bolt and Claude to generate a working mockup during the initial meeting. 

This immediate feedback loop:
* Creates instant alignment between business stakeholders and developers.
* Reduces lead times by catching design and logic flaws before a single line of production code is scheduled.
* Shifts the focus of early product development from "how to build" to "what to discover."

---

## Kenny Dendauw: Shifting from "Vibe" to "Value"
While Frank highlighted the rapid prototyping side, Kenny Dendauw provided a reality check from the perspective of a custom software development agency that has spent a decade delivering robust, production-grade applications.

AI tools allow developers to write code faster, but Kenny argued that **writing raw code was never the bottleneck in professional software delivery**. The real work is in understanding complex business workflows, designing scalable databases, ensuring strict security, and maintaining code over years.

### The Business Model Under Pressure
For years, agency models were built around charging for developer hours (time & material) or providing fixed quotes based on static specifications. With AI generating boilerplate code instantly:
* **Repricing**: Agencies must transition from selling "hours of coding" to selling "delivered value, architectural safety, and business alignment."
* **Client Expectations**: Clients expect features to be built instantly, requiring agencies to educate them on the difference between a prototype that "looks working" (vibe coding) and a system that is secure, compliant, and integrated into their ERP.
* **Engineering Guardrails**: Starfisk uses AI tools like Cursor and GitHub Copilot to accelerate development, but Kenny emphasized the strict guardrails they enforce: vetting generated code for security vulnerabilities, preventing intellectual property leakage, and maintaining a strict review process to avoid "AI spaghetti code."

---

## Conclusion: The Modern Developer Stack
The evening concluded with a lively networking session where attendees compared tool configurations and shared code generation workflows. 

Vibe coding is not replacing engineers; it is elevating them. The developers who thrive in 2026 are those who combine the speed of AI-driven prototyping with the traditional engineering rigor of software architecture, data integrity, and system safety.
